Dead of Night - Page 5

I gasped and its head whipped around to face me, the eye sockets empty and black. How could it see me? Did it see at all, or just hear? It seemed to consider its options, and I stayed as silent as I could, but it slowly got up off of its haunches, keeping its head turned towards me. The moonlight glinted off of the blood that dripped from its jaw, a long tongue flicked out and licked the blood off of its muzzle. I won’t let it get to my kids. I aimed my gun at its head, willing my hands to stop shaking. It started coming towards me, the antlers scraping the ceiling creating an ominous sound. I aimed, and pulled the trigger.

Five.

I missed. I immediately yanked the hammer back and pulled the trigger again.

Four.
