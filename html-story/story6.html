Dead of Night - Page 6

I missed again. The creature was startled by the noise, but by the second shot it had recovered and was lunging at me. I ducked behind the wall and cocked the gun again while running back into the kitchen. It came barreling around the corner into the hallway, and I aimed at its chest this time. I pulled the trigger.

Three.

It hit left of center and it went straight through its chest. The thing staggered back and fell to its knees but was very much still alive. I ran the other way and barreled through the kitchen and into the living room. Behind me, I heard its hooves pound against the tile and some shattered.

“Oh my god oh my god.” I kept running. Adrenaline shot through my limbs and I rounded back around into the foyer. I shot a look over my shoulder, which was a mistake because I saw that looming figure tearing after me. Its form backlit by the living room lamps and I couldn’t make out any of its features. I ran past the gory mess of flesh and bones, nothing was left of our sweet puppy.
